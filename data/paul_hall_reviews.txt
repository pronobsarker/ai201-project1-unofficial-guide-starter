DORM: Paul Hall
SOURCE: Roomsurf, The Gettysburgian, Gettysburg College Residential Education

REVIEW 1: Paul Hall is positioned in a highly convenient location on campus, though historically it was plagued by a lack of air conditioning, which caused the rooms to get extremely hot during summer.

REVIEW 2: The legacy infrastructure lacked a communal kitchen and suffered from thin walls, which often led to noisy, stressful nights and issues with hallway cleanliness during the weekends.

REVIEW 3: The administrative decision to force residents to pack up and vacate the building over winter break for major renovations caused significant stress during the final exam period.

PROS:
Undergoing complete physical modernisation to reopen in Fall 2026
- Unmatched central location close to classrooms, libraries, and the Dining Center
- Rooms feature highly spacious layout options

CONS:
Historically suffered from high noise levels, poor insulation, and a total lack of cooling
Pre-renovation building lacked basic kitchen amenities

BEST FOR: First-year students entering in Fall 2026 who want brand-new, modernised facilities in an ideal central location
