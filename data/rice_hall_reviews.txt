DORM: Rice Hall
SOURCE: Roomsurf, Gettysburg College Structured Data

REVIEW 1: Rice Hall is situated on the edge of the first-year residential quad, but its physical placement still allows students to reach any central academic building in under ten minutes.

REVIEW 2: While the inclusion of in-room air conditioning and microfridges is highly beneficial, the complete lack of a communal kitchen inside the building is a regular disappointment for students.

REVIEW 3: The physical layout feels somewhat institutional due to traditional cinder-block walls and the lack of an elevator, but it remains a solid and functional residential option.

PROS:
Full in-room air conditioning and standard MicroFridge units
Reliable first-floor laundry and garbage facilities
- Highly convenient walking times to central classes

CONS:
No kitchen facilities anywhere inside the building
- Lacks elevator access and is not ADA-compliant
Traditional cinder-block aesthetic can feel sterile and uninviting

BEST FOR: First-year students seeking a functional, quiet, air-conditioned home who do not require cooking facilities
