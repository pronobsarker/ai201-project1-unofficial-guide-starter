DORM: Hanson Hall
SOURCE: Roomsurf, Gettysburg College Residential Education

REVIEW 1: Hanson Hall provides significantly larger and more comfortable bedrooms compared to other traditional first-year options on campus, with the addition of reliable central air conditioning proving highly beneficial during warmer semesters.

REVIEW 2: The basement level is configured as a single-gender female floor, featuring a larger communal bathroom than upper levels, though it experiences periodic maintenance issues with clogged toilets and showers.

REVIEW 3: The social atmosphere is lively and highly collaborative, allowing first-year students to establish long-term friendships quickly through active community initiatives.

PROS:
Bedrooms are physically more spacious than adjacent first-year options
- Full central air conditioning throughout the structure
- Exceptionally social and welcoming environment

CONS:
Thin walls allow hallway conversations and noise to carry easily
Plumbing in the basement bathroom is prone to clogs and drainage delays
- Historical issues with laundry dryer ventilation

BEST FOR: First-year students who prioritize larger bedroom layouts and active social integration
