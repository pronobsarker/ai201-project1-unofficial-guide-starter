DORM: Latinx House
SOURCE: Gettysburg College Houses Directory, Gettysburg College Structured Data

REVIEW 1: Note: No direct student reviews were found. Officially, Latinx House (Carlisle House) focuses on diversifying the campus community and celebrating cultural differences.

REVIEW 2: Factual records describe the building as a spacious three-story house holding 24 students, with a full kitchen and large first-floor common space.

REVIEW 3: The house represents a key component of the college's growing network of safe-space cultural environments alongside Diaspora House.

PROS:
Extremely spacious first-floor common area and large full kitchen
- High-quality multicultural mission and supportive community
Includes dedicated first-floor gender-neutral bathrooms

CONS:
Completely lacks elevator access and ADA compliance
Multi-person bedrooms (triples and doubles) limit personal space

BEST FOR: Students passionate about Latinx culture, cultural advocacy, and living in a large, active family setting
