DORM: Huber Hall
SOURCE: WordPress (Huber Hall Student History Project), The Gettysburgian

REVIEW 1: Huber Hall possesses rich historical value, having functioned as a commercial hotel and a preparatory academy prior to its conversion into a student dormitory in 1999.

REVIEW 2: The physical layout is highly advantageous for first-year students because the hotel-style architecture guarantees larger bedrooms and integrated air conditioning.

REVIEW 3: While the building features excellent common spaces, such as second-floor kitchens and comfortable study lounges, co-ed dynamics are limited because the entire first floor remains strictly all-female.

PROS:
Features a second-floor communal kitchen and first-floor elevator
- Rooms are notably larger due to the building's legacy hotel layout
- Fully air-conditioned and ADA-compliant

CONS:
Bathroom facilities follow a traditional communal layout rather than private stalls
- Strictly single-gender on the first floor, reducing co-ed social flexibility

BEST FOR: First-year students seeking a historically charming, air-conditioned space with elevator access
