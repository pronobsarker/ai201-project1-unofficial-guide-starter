DORM: Mosaic House
SOURCE: Gettysburg College Houses Directory

REVIEW 1: Note: No direct student reviews were found. Officially, Mosaic House is structured around four pillars: Social Justice, Multiculturalism, Globalization, and Inclusion.

REVIEW 2: The house is a highly selective living-learning model where preference is given to students who demonstrate active social advocacy and activism.

REVIEW 3: Residents coordinate directly with the Office of Residential Education to foster positive community dialogue and global awareness on campus.

PROS:
Academically rich and intellectually stimulating environment
Focuses on tangible student advocacy and campus change

CONS:
Highly competitive and selective admission process
Small residential capacity restricts general availability

BEST FOR: Activists and socially driven students wanting to connect academic global topics directly with their living spaces
