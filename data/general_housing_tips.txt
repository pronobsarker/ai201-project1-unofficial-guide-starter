Gettysburg College Housing Process Tips
Credit-Based Housing Priorities: Gettysburg College uses a credit-based system to calculate lottery numbers. Rising Sophomores have their lottery numbers determined by Charting Your Course (CYC) credits, whereas rising Juniors utilize Sophomore Success Program (SSP) credits. Rising Seniors bypass credit metrics in favor of a completely randomized lottery system, and cumulative GPA is no longer factored into the lottery calculation for any undergraduate class.
Thematic Housing Relocations: The college is actively restructuring its thematic residential offerings. For the 2026-2027 academic year, the college is piloting "Independent Living Communities" (ILC) restricted to seniors. This program converts several long-standing themed houses—specifically Corner Cottage (Farm House), Lau (Blue Note), Smith (Writing House), and Van Horn (Ice Complex)—into independent senior residential units. This reshuffling has relocated established organizations (such as Farm House moving to Bregenzer, Art House to Hutchinson, and Blue Note merging with Harmony House at 209 N. Washington).
Inefficient Laundry Services: The campus laundry framework remains a primary source of student dissatisfaction. Under the current pay-per-use model, students can expect to pay over $100 per semester due to machine capacity limitations and mechanical inefficiencies that often require multiple drying cycles. Although the Student Senate has actively lobbied for a universally fee-free laundry system, students should currently budget extra financial resources for laundry or seek apartments (such as 209 N. Washington or Smyser) that feature superior in-building setups.
